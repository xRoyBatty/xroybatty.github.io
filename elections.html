Definitions
A The period, typically 24 hours before and during election day, when campaigning is prohibited and no new polls or electoral predictions may be published
B The percentage of eligible voters who actually cast ballots in an election
C The lower house of the Polish parliament, consisting of 460 members elected to four-year terms
D An official location where voters go to cast their ballots during an election
E A voting system used to elect the President of Poland in which a second round is held if no candidate receives an absolute majority in the first round
F The minimum percentage of votes that a political party needs to secure representation in the parliament (5% for single parties, 8% for coalitions in Poland)
G The official body responsible for overseeing elections, verifying candidates, and certifying results in Poland
H Elections to select members of both chambers of the Polish parliament (Sejm and Senate)
I The upper house of the Polish parliament, comprising 100 senators elected in single-member districts
J A short advertisement created by a political candidate to promote their candidacy during election campaigns
K The official list of people eligible to vote in a particular electoral district
L A proportional representation system used in Polish parliamentary elections to convert votes into seats by dividing the votes for each party by successive divisors
M A geographical area that elects representatives to the parliament; Poland has 41 constituencies for Sejm elections
N The direct election by citizens to choose the President of Poland, who serves a five-year term and can be re-elected only once
O An officially registered group that has the right to nominate candidates for election, can be formed by political parties, voters, or organizations
