Lesson Plan: Politics and Deception

Objectives:

  • Students will be able to understand and use vocabulary related to politics and deception.
  • Students will be able to comprehend a text about political accusations of deception.
  • Students will be able to discuss the topic of political deception and express their opinions.

Gap-filling task:

Accusations of Deception Fly in Polish Politics

The leader of Poland’s ruling Law and Justice party (PiS), Jarosław Kaczyński, has 1. __________ the current government coalition of “deception” during a recent rally. Kaczyński 2. __________ that the opposition’s campaign promises were “a multi-layered deception” aimed at misleading the Polish people. He specifically 3. __________ the opposition for making false promises about the economy and the daily lives of Poles. According to Kaczyński, during its eight years in power, PiS 4. __________ Poland from a “third-world country” to a “civilized state” by, among other things, addressing the issue of child poverty. He 5. __________ that this was achieved despite opposition claims of insufficient funds. Kaczyński also 6. __________ the opposition for failing to deliver on its 100-day plan, stating that only nine out of 100 promises have been 7. __________. He argued that this demonstrates the opposition's 8. __________ to deceive the public. Turning to the issue of national security, Kaczyński questioned whether Poland should be a strong and independent nation or 9. __________ on Germany and, increasingly, Russia. He contrasted his party's goal of making Poland a leading force in the European Union with the current government's perceived weakness on security matters. Kaczyński specifically criticized the former Prime Minister Donald Tusk for his 10. __________ on Russia’s actions on the Polish border, claiming that Tusk has shifted his position from downplaying the threat to now acknowledging it as “hybrid warfare.” Kaczyński concluded by asserting that Tusk is not a reliable guarantor of national security.

Text comprehension questions:

  1. Who is Jarosław Kaczyński?
  2. What did Kaczyński accuse the current government coalition of?
  3. What specific campaign promises did Kaczyński criticize the opposition for making?
  4. According to Kaczyński, how did PiS improve Poland during its time in power?
  5. What is Kaczyński's view on Poland’s relationship with Germany and Russia?
  6. What is Kaczyński's opinion of Donald Tusk's stance on Russia?
  7. Why did Kaczyński criticize the opposition's 100-day plan?
  8. How does Kaczyński view the current government's handling of national security?
  9. What is meant by the term “hybrid warfare”?
  10. What is your overall impression of Kaczyński's accusations?

Discussion questions:

  1. Do you think Kaczyński's accusations against the opposition are justified? Why or why not?
  2. What do you think motivates politicians to make accusations against their opponents?
  3. Is it important for politicians to be truthful? Why or why not?
  4. What are the potential consequences of political deception?
  5. How can citizens protect themselves from being misled by politicians?
  6. Do you think the media plays a role in promoting or preventing political deception?
  7. What are some examples of political deception from your own country?
  8. What measures can be taken to increase transparency and accountability in government?
  9. How can we encourage more civil and respectful discourse in politics?
  10. Do you think the truth always comes out in the end? Why or why not?
