List Formalny: Wolontariat dla Seniorów

Dear University’s Community Outreach Coordinator, I want to express my concerns about the issue of social isolation of elderly in our town. Old people tend to fall into many illnesses that distressingly contribute to their usual lifestyle. Nurseries provide high quality health care, but mental health is often put aside. My intention is to suggest a solution to this problem. Starting a student volunteer program, which supports local seniors might have an impressive impact on the social isolation of elderly. During high school I was a volunteer at my local Nursery home. My duties were to ensure entertainment and keep them company. A once in a while escape from boredom, that is in a nursery home, turned out to be a cure for their sadness. Many of them had lost their loved ones or their family abandoned them due to their sickness. Consequently they were put up with not only physical pain but also mental pain. This experience has gained me a lot of knowledge and empathy as well as the elderly had their benefits of having me there. I believe all of the students on our campus should have a chance to make someone’s day better. Students could play card or board games with pensioners, or do signing classes. Every activity would be valuable as long as they are suited for elderly physical condition. Art classes, manual activities or various types of quizzes are what older people crave the most! Students could go t0 a local Nursery home after classes and by these simple activities, they could have a relevant impact on the social life of elderly. Furthermore, students would gain consciousness about the importance of caring for others, they would gain more empathy and a will to help. Meanwhile, seniors would have a more joyful time which profits their mental health. Last but not least, our school would have a fantastic public image of emphatic socially working students. I hope you take my suggestion into consideration. Yours faithfully, XYZ

List do Redakcji: Siłownie Plenerowe

To whom it may concern, I am writing to you to share my point of view on outdoor gyms. As I talked to people, I came to the conclusion that there are too many open air gyms when the town’s budget can offer other various types of activities. In my opinion the area of the gyms could be used differently. As an individual, I have seen machines in outdoor gyms that practically don’t make sense, when it comes to a sporty lifestyle. For instance, a machine that’s meant probably for weight lifting. People sit on the bench and with arms lift the weight of their own body. As I talked to a physiotherapist, it turned out that it is impossible to build muscle on this machine. Moreover, the correct position of the body that should be maintained is disturbed. Thus, long term practice on that machine might lead to injuries. On the other hand, some machines are practical. The pull-up bar is very useful for people who during their cardio practice, want to stop for a stretch or some light lifting exercise. Nonetheless, outdoor gyms are mostly bustling with children who treat it like a playground. In that case, the gym’s purpose is not fulfilled. Instead of building other pointless outdoor gyms, the area can be used differently. In local park could be build a playground for dogs. People living in a block of flats do not have a lot of choices when it comes to going for a walk with their dog. The park might seem like an ideal place, moreover, the dogs would have their special area where they could run and play leash-free. The place would be surrounded by a fence, thus they don’t run away and dog owners would be assured about their pupil’s safety. Outdoor public swimming pools can also have fantastic usage especially for people who can not afford proper air conditioning for summer. It would be exciting for the kids to have a place where they can meet with their friends to play in the water. Yours faithfully, XYZ
